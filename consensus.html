Consensus: The Antidote to AI Hallucinations

The biggest problem with the current generation of AI models is that they are extremely confident liars. If you are doing serious market research, drafting a policy, or looking for medical insights, asking ChatGPT to summarize a topic is playing Russian roulette with your credibility. It will hallucinate facts and invent sources just to please you.

Research Backed by Science

We need tools that separate fact from algorithmically generated fiction. Consensus is an AI search engine that doesn't just scrape the web; it searches exclusively through peer-reviewed, published scientific papers. It is the tool I use when the stakes are high and "mostly correct" isn't good enough.

  • No Hallucinated Sources: Every single claim Consensus makes is tied directly to a real, citable scientific paper. If the research doesn't exist, Consensus won't invent it.
  • The 'Consensus Meter': When you ask a yes/no question (e.g., "Does intermittent fasting improve focus?"), it analyzes the bulk of the available literature and gives you a visual breakdown of where the scientific community stands.
  • Synthesis Without the Spin: It strips away the academic jargon and gives you the core findings, allowing you to process heavy research papers in minutes rather than hours.

The Edge Perspective

In a world drowning in synthetic content, verified truth is becoming the most valuable currency. If your business or research relies on absolute accuracy, Consensus is non-negotiable infrastructure.
